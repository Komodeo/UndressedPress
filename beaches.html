What nudists say about Wales's best nude beaches

by Demi Roberts

It might still be winter, but cold water skinny dipping is very much on trend

We all like to be beside the seaside - but there are some brave folk out there who like the experience to be even more, ahem, natural.

The wind in their hair, the breeze between the knees - and the sun on their buns - and there are a handful nudist beaches in Wales where you can do just that.

Some are quite well known, others are just for those "in the know" - and there are often a number of local rules and regulations in place.

According to the Crown Prosecution Service, naturists shouldn't be prosecuted unless they are involved in an offence other than public nudity. It says a balance needs to be struck between the naturist's right to freedom of expression and the right of the wider public to be protected from harassment, alarm and distress.

So, with that in mind, we took at look at how nudists and naturists really felt about stripping down to the skin on the Welsh coast.

1. Whiteford Sands, Gower

Whiteford Sands is the most northernly beach on Gower, located on the edge of the Loughor estuary.

The beach is well-known for being a naturist favourite, but according to a community forum, local residents are said 'not to approve' of such activities.

With that being said, at two miles long, Whiteford Sands is very spacious, meaning you'll probably get more privacy than you'd expect.

A reviewer on Naturist Guide said that some nudists at Whiteford Sands were "very chatty and extremely friendly".

They said: "The only others I saw were a couple (clothed) and a single man on the flattish sand dunes just past the lighthouse. He stripped off when he saw me and turned out to be a very chatty and extremely friendly guy. I saw no-one else at all! Quite amazing considering the size of the beach.
